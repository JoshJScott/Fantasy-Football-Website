I.���������� b.� Payout Structure

�������������������������� The concept of the variable buy-in functions like players going all in during a poker game. SKIP the rest of this paragraph if you know how all-in betting in poker takes place. If multiple players go all-in, the person with the smallest chip stack can not take all the money of the person with the largest chip stack. Instead, someone who goes all in with $10 can only win $10 from everyone else who goes all-in. This person can take all of someone�s money if they went all-in for 8$, but if someone goes all in for $12 dollars, our original $10 better is not even playing for those last $2. Only people who have bet $12 or more can play for the full $12. So let�s say $12 is the max bet for this pot. If a $12 better wins, they get all the money. BUT, if a lower better wins, they only win what they paid in for, and then the remaining money goes to the person who has the best hand out of the people who put in more money than the actual winner. Thus, if the two highest betters were in last and 2nd to last place, the guy who got second to last would win any money from the last place better that didn�t go to everyone else (because everyone else put in less money, and has no right to play for the additional amount that the high betters put in above them)

In poker though, winner takes all. In fantasy, there are many payouts. Thus, the amount you are �playing for� can be understood simply as the entire pot you have a right to compete for, and your winnings will be a percentage of that pot based on these percentages.
